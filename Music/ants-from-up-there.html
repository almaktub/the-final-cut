✈️
The Concorde Problem
The Concorde was beautiful, fast, and impossible - cancelled in 2003 because it was too loud and the economics never worked. Isaac Wood uses it throughout as a metaphor for things he wants that are structurally unworkable: the relationship, his ambitions, himself. The album's title says it plainly. From up high, we all look like ants. The Concorde knew this.
📞
Long-Distance Love as Grief
The relationship at the album's center is ending during the songs. He's narrating the collapse from inside the middle of it - the distance, the flights, the moments where the gap becomes visible. "Bread Song" is the hinge: he's making bread; she's in the air. The domestic and the impossible in the same frame. The grief is already present before anything formally ends.
🗜️
The Clamp
A recurring image - something that holds, constrains, prevents. Never fully defined. The clamp is whatever stops him. The therapist. Himself. The distance. The expectation. The album never pins it down because it doesn't need to. You know what your clamp is. His is just more orchestrated.
📖
Literary Self-Awareness
"I'll be the best there's ever been" - he knows how that sounds. That's the point. The album is full of this: Isaac narrating his own grandiosity from inside it, with enough distance to see the absurdity and enough investment to mean it anyway. It's funnier than it should be and more devastating than you expect.

Things You Didn't Know
01
The Four-Day Gap
On January 31, 2022 - four days before the album's release - Isaac Wood posted a handwritten note announcing his departure from the band for mental health reasons. The remaining six members decided to release the album anyway. This is the right call and the only possible call: the album had already been made and it was extraordinary.
02
The Concorde's Actual Timeline
The Concorde made its first commercial flight in 1976. It was retired in 2003 after an Air France crash in 2000. The aircraft was cancelled because it was too loud for most airports, too expensive to run commercially, and there weren't enough wealthy passengers willing to pay. Isaac Wood was born in 2001. He never flew on one. Neither did most people.
03
What BCNR Did Next
The remaining members continued as Black Country, New Road - no single frontperson, vocals distributed across the group. They released Live at Bush Hall in 2022 and Ants (2023), both critically praised on their own terms. The band survived the departure by becoming a different kind of band. Whether Ants From Up There would exist without Isaac Wood as its author is not a question worth asking. It does exist. That's enough.
Track by Track
The Dissection
02Chaos Space Marine
8.8▼
The album's first full statement after the intro - big, orchestrated, a little absurd in the best way. The title is from Warhammer 40K. The song is about something much closer to home. Isaac's particular gift: the literary reference that unlocks the personal rather than hiding it.
03Concorde
9.6▼
The thesis track and the album's first peak. Orchestral, building, desperate, and gorgeous. The Concorde as the beautiful impossible thing. The relationship as the Concorde. Isaac as the person standing at the gate after it's already left. The arrangement earns every instrument it deploys.
04Bread Song
9.3▼
The emotional hinge of the album. He's at home making bread; she's on a plane. Domestic labor and impossible distance in the same frame. The bread will be ready. She won't be there. This is the most intimate track on the record and it works because it's specific about what love in distance looks like on an ordinary Tuesday.
05Good Will Hunting
9.0▼
The title is doing something funny and the song is doing something devastating. The 1997 film is invoked as a shared reference point, which is to say: a specific kind of emotional intelligence that can articulate exactly what's wrong and still be unable to fix it. That's also this album's condition.
10Basketball Shoes
9.8▼
Nine minutes and twelve seconds. The closer. Isaac tells you directly: he knows what he's doing and he's doing it anyway. "I'll be the best there's ever been" - in context, this isn't arrogance. It's a man narrating his own delusion in real time, with full knowledge that it is a delusion, and meaning every word of it. One of the best closers of the decade. The album earns this ending.

Footnote
This album does not have weak links. It has links of varying intensity, which is a different thing. The distinction matters because the lower-scoring tracks serve purposes that the high-scoring tracks couldn't - pacing, breath, contrast. An album that was all Concorde and Basketball Shoes would be exhausting. Haldern and Mark's Theme make the peaks possible. The sequencing knows what it's doing.
