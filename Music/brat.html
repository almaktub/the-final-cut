Overall Score
"A record about being too much - too loud, too needy, too honest about all of it - that became the most culturally omnipresent thing of 2024."
8.7 because Brat does something genuinely hard: it makes anxiety and self-obsession feel communal rather than alienating. Charli has been making music for fifteen years and the mainstream kept almost arriving. This time it did, not because she compromised but because the production found a pocket where abrasion and accessibility sit next to each other without either one backing down. Girl, so confusing and 365 are the two tracks that will outlast the moment. The 1.3: the middle section sags, and a few cuts that work as mood pieces in sequence don't have the internal momentum to survive a standalone listen. But the ceiling on this record is high, and the floor is higher than most pop albums manage.

The Brat Identity
Brat isn't an aesthetic. It's a refusal to manage how you come across. Charli builds an entire record out of the specific discomfort of caring too much about not seeming like you care - and finding liberation in dropping the act.
👯
Friendship as Currency
The album's emotional center isn't romance - it's female friendship, competition, and the specific terror of being evaluated by people whose opinion you've decided matters. Girl, so confusing puts that directly in the room and names it.
🏆
Fame's Hollow Core
Charli has been adjacent to fame long enough to understand it without believing in it. I think about it all the time is the most emotionally honest track on the record - a woman standing in the middle of everything she wanted, genuinely unsure if it was worth it.
🔊
Anxiety Performed Loudly
The production strategy is the thesis: if you're going to fall apart, fall apart at 140 BPM. The abrasive textures aren't hiding vulnerability. They're what vulnerability sounds like when it stops apologizing for itself.

The opener announces everything: the mode, the posture, the production language. 'I'm everywhere' as both threat and confession. Clavish's verse is the album's best guest moment and it arrives before you've settled in.

A nostalgia track that refuses to be comfortable. The specific ache of a generation that grew up on music that's now heritage is real and she doesn't over-explain it.
03Sympathy is a knife
9.0▼
The sharpest lyric on the album and one of the more quietly devastating pop songs of 2024. The title does the work but the verses earn it. Envy and love pressed so close together they've become the same object.
04I might say something stupid
8.4▼
A mood track more than a song. Works in sequence. The production wraps around the anxiety of speaking before thinking in a way that makes the structure feel like the subject.
05Talk talk
9.1▼
The album's most purely fun moment. The production breathes in a way most of the record doesn't and the result is a club track with actual edge. The Talk Talk name-check is earned.
06Von dutch
8.9▼
The lead single that launched the era. The Y2K fashion reference is bait - the actual content is about being abandoned by someone who couldn't handle what you are. The production is meaner than it sounds on first pass.
07Girl, so confusing
9.3▼
The emotional peak. Named but not directed - a song about a specific friendship rivalry that generalizes perfectly. 'I heard her when she cries, she heard me when I cried' is the most human line on the record. The Lorde remix extended it into something richer but the original had the nerve first.
08Apple
8.6▼
A family inheritance song that doesn't sentimentalize the inheritance. 'The apple don't fall far from the tree' as self-indictment. Compact and cold.
09B2b
8.7▼
A sex track with more sadness in the production than the lyrics admit to. The back-to-back structure implies something is being avoided rather than enjoyed. A minor standout.
10Mean girls
8.5▼
Treads some familiar ground but the self-implication redeems it. She's not singing about mean girls as an outside observer. The self-awareness is the point.
11I think about it all the time
9.0▼
The album drops the distortion for three minutes and it earns every second of quiet. A woman in her thirties standing at the edge of a decision about children and legacy, genuinely unsure. The most vulnerable thing on the record because the production can't protect it.
12365
9.1▼
The closing statement and the album in one track: wanting to be seen as a version of yourself that might not be accurate, and deciding the wanting is enough. Goes out loud. The right call.

Footnote
Charli recorded Brat while her close friend and collaborator SOPHIE was no longer alive. The album doesn't announce the grief but it's in the production - an abrasiveness that keeps the listener slightly off-balance, never quite comfortable. The lime green cover was an intentional provocation. It worked.
