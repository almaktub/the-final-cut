Overall Score
"The anti-SIMBI. Stripped back, rawer, more personal. A lot of artists would have tried to top themselves. Simz made something quieter instead, and it was the right call."
9.2 because No Thank You is a record about what it costs to make records - specifically what the last few years cost Little Simz personally, professionally, and creatively. Gorilla opens with her on the track alone, no orchestra, no pageantry: just the sharpest rapper in the room stating the terms. His Story, Broken, and Sideways are the emotional core, three tracks where the writing is more specific and more unguarded than most of SIMBI because the scale of the production isn't carrying any of the weight. The 0.8 missing: by design, it doesn't hit as hard. The ceiling is lower and the album knows it. A few tracks feel like sketches that didn't quite finish developing. But Gorilla alone made No Thank You worth releasing, and Broken is the most vulnerable she has put on record since I Love You, I Hate You. Some albums exist to prove an artist is still in control. This is one of those albums.

Restraint as Statement
SIMBI had an orchestra. No Thank You has almost nothing. The stripped-back production is not a budget decision - it's an argument. Little Simz is proving that the writing was always the thing, that the strings on SIMBI were a frame for something that doesn't need a frame. No Thank You is the same artist without the scaffolding.
💰
The Cost of Success
The album is partly about what happens after you win the Mercury Prize and the BRIT and the critical consensus - the expectation, the people who appear, the relationships that shift. No Thank You is Simz sorting through what the last two years actually were, away from the awards and the press cycle.
🎚️
Raw Production as Honesty
Inflo's production on No Thank You is deliberately sparse. Where SIMBI filled every available space, this album leaves gaps. The gaps are not empty - they're where the listener is supposed to be. A more collaborative record, in the sense that the listener has to do more work to meet it.
🧭
Post-peak Clarity
Released nine months after SIMBI, No Thank You is an artist recalibrating rather than ascending. The urgency is different - not the urgency of proving yourself but the urgency of staying honest when everyone is watching. It's a harder thing to make and a subtler thing to appreciate.

Track by Track
The Dissection
01 Gorilla
9.4 ▼
The mission statement. No build-up, no orchestra - just Little Simz on a beat, the most confident she has sounded on a first track since Introvert. The production is lean and the performance is not. The album front-loads its best argument and trusts the rest of the record to hold up.
02 Silvetrini
8.8 ▼
Named after her manager. An act of loyalty dressed as a rap track. The production is warm in a way the album rarely allows itself. A strong second track that doesn't try to outdo Gorilla and is smarter for it.
03 His Story
9.2 ▼
The album's most lyrically dense track. Simz taking on a perspective not her own with enough precision that the track never feels like an exercise. The writing here is the most controlled on the album.
04 Sideways
9.1 ▼
The emotional terrain of the album mapped in one track. Things going wrong in a specific direction rather than a general one. The production gives the vocals space and the vocals use it. One of the three best tracks here.
05 No Merci
8.7 ▼
French-inflected production that gives the album one of its few moments of levity - though Simz is not exactly lightening up, she is giving the listener a different posture to adopt for three minutes. Works as a tonal break.
06 Broken
9.3 ▼
The album's most unguarded moment. Simz on what the last two years actually felt like beneath the awards and the critical praise. The vulnerability here is the same register as I Love You, I Hate You but quieter and more deliberate. The album's second-best track.
07 Lotus Flower
8.5 ▼
A track that knows what it is: a moment of relative gentleness in an album that doesn't have many. The production floats. Simz allows herself to be less precise than usual. A nice track that belongs on a slightly different record.
08 Angel
8.6 ▼
A late-album quiet track. The production is stripped to almost nothing. Simz at her most understated. The track works as a come-down but the album could have ended one track earlier and been slightly stronger for it.
09 Venom
8.9 ▼
The correct closer. Simz returning to the directness of Gorilla for the final minutes. The album ending the way it started - on her terms, in her own words, with no assistance. The circle closes.

Footnote
No Thank You was released in December 2022, nine months after Sometimes I Might Be Introvert won the Mercury Prize and the BRIT Award for Album of the Year. Little Simz has said the album was made quickly and intentionally - she did not want to spend years crafting a follow-up. The title is a direct response to a period in which people around her wanted things she wasn't willing to give. The album received less press than SIMBI but comparable critical scores. It is the kind of record that only makes sense after the one before it.
