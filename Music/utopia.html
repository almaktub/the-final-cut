Overall Score
"Travis in maximalist mode, throwing every collaborator at a wall and seeing what sticks. More of it works than doesn't, but the ratio is tighter than it should be at 17 tracks."
8.4 because Utopia is three different albums fighting for the same runtime. HYAENA is a statement of intent: aggressive, textured, and strange in all the right ways. PARASAIL is what happens when Travis finds the pocket between ambient and rap and refuses to move. MODERN JAM with Pharrell is the most fun either of them has sounded in years. FE!N with Playboi Carti is genuinely chaotic in the way Travis's best work tends to be. CIRCUS MAXIMUS runs eight minutes and earns most of them. And then there's MELTDOWN - Drake doing Drake things over a beat that deserves better - and POPSTAR, which sounds like a contractual obligation, and K-POP, which is exactly what it sounds like. The 1.6 missing: Utopia is a great hour of music masquerading as a great album. They are not the same thing. The edit that turns this into a 9.0 record exists. Travis didn't make it.

Utopia as Escape
The title is ironic by design. Utopia is an impossible place, and Travis is clearly aware that he's building one in the same way he built Astroworld - out of memory, loss, and the desire to exist somewhere else. The album's production creates spaces that feel unreachable. That's the point.
🔊
Maximalism as Statement
Where most producers scale back after a setback, Travis scaled up. Utopia is louder, more crowded, more ambitious in its feature list than anything he's made. The message is that he's still here and he's still operating at this level. Whether the message lands depends on which ten tracks you're listening to.
🤝
The Collaborator Network
Beyonce, Bad Bunny, Drake, Playboi Carti, The Weeknd, Swae Lee, 21 Savage, Pharrell, Yung Lean, Pop Smoke, Westside Gunn, James Blake. Travis treats the album as a hub. Some of these collabs produce the album's best moments. Some produce its most forgettable ones. The gap between the two is wider than on Astroworld.
🕯️
Grief Displaced into Sound
Utopia arrived nearly two years after the Astroworld Festival tragedy. Travis never directly addresses it but the album is shot through with an energy that reads as something unresolved. The maximalism might be processing. The scale might be overcompensation. Or it might just be an ambitious album. All three interpretations hold.

Track by Track
The Dissection
01 HYAENA
9.2 ▼
The right opening. Aggressive without being obvious, textured without being fussy. Travis announcing that whatever happened, this is still what he sounds like. The production is doing architectural work and the track knows it.
02 THANK GOD (feat. Baby Keem)
8.5 ▼
Baby Keem arrives and does Baby Keem things at a level that makes him the better performer here. A strong track that works as a handoff between HYAENA's aggression and the middle section.
03 MODERN JAM (feat. Pharrell Williams)
9.1 ▼
Pharrell sounds genuinely alive here in a way he hasn't on a Travis record before. The production is warm, slightly retro, completely at odds with everything around it - which is exactly why it works. One of the album's best stretches.
04 MY EYES
8.6 ▼
Travis finding the melodic pocket he lives in best. A strong mid-album track that doesn't ask too much and delivers what it promises. Sequenced well against MODERN JAM.
05 GODS COUNTRY
8.4 ▼
Atmospheric and patient. The album allowing itself a brief moment of stillness before the feature-heavy second half. Good without being indispensable.
06 DELRESTO (ECHOES) (feat. Beyoncé)
8.8 ▼
Beyonce appears and the album briefly becomes something else. Her presence doesn't overpower the track - she fits into the sonic space Travis built rather than redecorating it. One of the more surprising collabs on any major rap album in recent memory.
07 I KNOW ? (feat. Bad Bunny)
8.3 ▼
Bad Bunny doing Bad Bunny. The track is good but the collaboration doesn't add up to more than the sum of its parts. Fine in the sequence, forgettable outside it.
08 MELTDOWN (feat. Drake)
8.1 ▼
Drake's verse is competent and the beat around it is genuinely interesting. The problem is that MELTDOWN sounds like a placeholder where a more unexpected choice should have gone. The most anticipated collab on the album is also its most predictable.
09 FE!N (feat. Playboi Carti)
9.0 ▼
Carti and Travis at maximum chaos, a beat that sounds like a server overheating. It became a cultural moment for a reason. The album needed at least one track that felt genuinely dangerous and this is it.
10 LOST FOREVER (feat. Westside Gunn, Swae Lee, James Blake)
8.2 ▼
The most eclectic combination of features on the album. James Blake's presence pulls it toward something interesting. Westside Gunn sounds slightly out of context. Swae Lee is Swae Lee. The track is more interesting as a concept than as a listen.
11 PARASAIL (feat. Yung Lean, Wheezy)
9.0 ▼
The album's most patient moment. Floats rather than propels. Yung Lean was the correct choice for a track this atmospheric - his delivery suits the production's temperature exactly. One of the best things here.
12 TOPIA TWINS (feat. Rob49, 21 Savage)
8.0 ▼
21 Savage sounds comfortable. Rob49 does what the track needs him to do. Competent rap music in the middle of an album that keeps promising something stranger.
13 CIRCUS MAXIMUS (feat. The Weeknd, Swae Lee)
8.7 ▼
Eight minutes and it mostly earns them. The Weeknd arrives at the four-minute mark and the track shifts into something else. One of the more genuinely ambitious pieces here - a rap-pop psychedelic structure that doesn't apologize for its length.
14 POPSTAR (feat. Pop Smoke, Quavo)
7.6 ▼
The weakest track. Feels assembled rather than made. Pop Smoke's inclusion is posthumous and the track doesn't do his catalog justice. Quavo is Quavo. The production is fine. The whole thing sounds like what it is: a late-album filler with famous names attached.
15 K-POP (feat. Bad Bunny, The Weeknd)
7.8 ▼
Does exactly what the title suggests. Fun enough but the album doesn't need two Bad Bunny appearances and this is the weaker of the two. The Weeknd is better served by CIRCUS MAXIMUS.
16 TELEKINESIS (feat. SoFaygo)
8.3 ▼
The album recalibrating toward its close. SoFaygo fits the production naturally. A decent late-album track that doesn't waste its placement.
17 SIRENS
8.5 ▼
The right closer. Cinematic, unhurried, Travis leaving a long silence before the end. The album needed a closer that felt like landing and this is it. Better than most of the tracks that came before it in the last third.

Footnote
Utopia was released on July 28, 2023, almost two years after the Astroworld Festival tragedy in November 2021. Travis Scott had largely been absent from mainstream releases in the interim. The album debuted at number one in multiple countries and was accompanied by a film directed by Gaspar Noe. It was the most anticipated rap release of 2023 and arrived to a divided critical response - praised for its ambition, questioned for its length. The conversation around it has been inseparable from the circumstances of its making.
