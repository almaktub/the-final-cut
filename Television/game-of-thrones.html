Final Cut TV No. 03

Game of Thrones

creators D.B. Weiss & David Benioff  ·  2011–2019  ·  Fantasy  ·  8 Seasons
8.6
Out of ten
Six seasons of the best political drama television has made, followed by two seasons that ran out of book and out of patience.

For six seasons, Game of Thrones did something almost nobody else attempts: it treated fantasy as a genre for adults arguing about power, not children fighting dragons. The best of it, the Red Wedding, Tyrion's trial, Cersei's walk of shame, works because the show understood the most frightening thing in Westeros was never the White Walkers. It was people making rational decisions in their own self-interest at everyone else's expense. Ned Stark loses his head in episode nine of season one, and the show spends the next several years living inside the vacuum that decision created. That's a structural gamble almost no other show would take, and it pays off completely.

The show also assembled the best ensemble television has managed outside The Wire and The Sopranos, and it used the sprawl honestly: nobody was safe, no storyline existed just to serve a lead. Tyrion, Arya, Jaime, Brienne, characters who could have stayed simple archetypes instead earned real reversals. Then the show ran out of book. Once it passed George R.R. Martin's published material, the writers had years of accumulated character work and no map, and it shows. People who spent seasons earning their arcs get compressed into a season and a half of shortcuts. Dany's turn especially needed another two years to land the way the first six seasons would have landed it.

The 8.6 is the honest math: six seasons at a 9.5-10 level, two seasons that undercut a lot of what came before, averaged and then nudged up because even the worst stretch of this show is still better than most television's best. It's not a show ruined by its ending so much as a show that proved, twice, what happens when you write eight years of consequence and then run out of time to pay it off properly.

"When you play the game of thrones, you win or you die. There is no middle ground."
Cersei Lannister — Game of Thrones
Rewatch verdict
Rewatch It
The first six seasons are as good as television gets. Rewatch those and let your memory finish the story better than the last two did.
