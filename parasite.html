Final Cut No. 1

Parasite

dir. Bong Joon-ho  ·  2019  ·  Thriller  ·  132 min
9.5
Out of ten
Class war as genre exercise, perfectly engineered so you notice the trap only after it's sprung.

The film's thesis is delivered as a throwaway line: "She's nice because she's rich." Not rich and nice — rich, therefore nice. The Park family has the generosity that comes free with the purchase of never needing anything. The Kim family has the resourcefulness that comes from needing everything. Bong Joon-ho builds the two households as architectural arguments: the Kim semi-basement floods; the Park glass house catches all the light. Staircases do more dramatic work in this film than most directors get from their entire cast.

The first hour is a caper comedy and it's an immaculate one. The Kims infiltrate the Parks one by one, each new member displacing someone via a carefully engineered scandal, and the plan is so clean you cheer for them even knowing what cheering means. Then the housekeeper rings the doorbell in the rain. The film's second half is a different movie: darker, faster, and finally violent in ways that the first half earned through restraint. Bong withholds the basement until the moment it explodes the genre underneath everything. The smell — the recurring reference to the Kim family's particular odor that the Parks discuss without noticing the Kims are in the room — is the film's sharpest instrument. Class as something literally on the skin, something that a good address cannot wash off.

The 9.5 rather than a 10: the Parks are thin by design, and that thinness occasionally tips from intentional to convenient. Ki-taek's garden party kill lands hard, but a few beats before it feel schematic. The film survives it because Bong trusts his architecture. The ending is a letter written by a son to a father who will never read it, and it's the cruelest possible happy ending: the plan works and changes nothing.

"She's nice because she's rich. If I had all this money, I'd be nice too."
Ki-woo Kim — Parasite
Rewatch verdict
Rewatch It
The first watch is the trap. The second is watching Bong lay it — every detail in the first hour that doubles as a loaded weapon for the second.
